# Player Journey

## Purpose

This document maps the expected player path from first launch through post-game play. It identifies what the player is trying to accomplish, which systems should be available, where the current experience can become unclear or repetitive, and what improvements would make each phase easier to understand and more rewarding.

## Journey overview

| Stage | Approximate timing | Core promise | Primary success signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Game start | First launch to first planted seed | Become a new orchard caretaker with a clear first action. | Player plants a starter crop without needing to inspect every tab. |
| 2. Tutorial | First planted seed to first harvest | Learn the daily loop: plant, care, advance day, react to weather, harvest. | Player completes a guided first-harvest chain. |
| 3. First hour | First harvest through early upgrades | Convert harvests into visible power and collection progress. | Player buys at least one upgrade and unlocks several archive entries. |
| 4. First day | Several sessions or one extended session | Establish a routine with goals that survive logout. | Player understands forecasts, orchard expansion, market use, and account persistence. |
| 5. Mid game | After core loop mastery | Choose specialties and expand production capacity. | Player unlocks extra orchards and pursues crop, weather, and economy objectives. |
| 6. Late game | Most systems unlocked | Optimize, automate, complete collections, and chase mastery. | Player has a portfolio of long-term targets beyond earning more credits. |
| 7. End game | Completion of major goals | Resolve the main progression arc and celebrate mastery. | Player receives a clear completion moment with permanent recognition. |
| 8. Post game | After completion | Keep returning for mastery, experiments, events, and social comparison. | Player has repeatable optional goals that do not feel mandatory or punitive. |

## 1. Game start

### Player goals

- Understand the fantasy: they are operating a sci-fi orchard and botanical archive.
- Identify the first actionable step without needing prior knowledge.
- Spend starting credits safely on a starter seed.
- See that the orchard grid, plant detail panel, weather, tabs, credits, and Data Seeds are important.

### Expected unlocked systems

- Account/login entry point or guest start.
- Day counter, starting credits, and empty Data Seeds balance.
- Starter orchard with open plots.
- Seed purchase and plant selection.
- Basic plant telemetry: growth, water, nutrients, stress, pests, burning, and harvest readiness.
- Current weather and short forecast.
- Navigation tabs for orchard, lab, market, rankings, archives, and profile, even if some are too advanced for the first minute.

### Rewards

- Immediate visual confirmation when a plot is planted.
- Starter crop identity, name, and early lore flavor.
- First log message confirming successful planting.
- A clear sense that the orchard is no longer empty.

### Risks of confusion or drop-off

- Too many tabs are visible before the player knows why they matter.
- Starting resources may look like generic numbers rather than actionable budgets.
- A player can inspect locked, advanced, or empty systems before learning the core loop.
- Weather may appear important but not explain how it changes plant outcomes.
- The game may not make it obvious whether time passes automatically or through manual day advancement.

### Boring loops

- If the player plants once and only waits for growth, the opening can feel passive.
- Repeatedly clicking through panels before a plant exists offers little meaning.

### Missing feedback

- No prominent first-objective callout such as “Plant your first Terran Sprout.”
- No cost preview showing what the seed purchase enables and what remains affordable.
- Limited explanation of why the selected crop is safe for beginners.

### Missing rewards

- No dedicated “first planting” badge, bonus, or narrative beat.
- No starter kit reward for completing the first action.

### Progression weaknesses

- The game start exposes breadth before establishing sequence.
- Progression appears currency-led before the player understands crop mastery, archive completion, or upgrades.

### Suggested improvements

- Add a first-run objective card with one action at a time: select plot, buy seed, plant seed.
- Temporarily highlight the orchard grid and seed purchase affordance before highlighting other tabs.
- Add a starter seed recommendation and “safe first choice” label.
- Add a planting completion toast with a small credit rebate, lore snippet, or archive teaser.
- Gate or visually de-emphasize advanced tabs until the first crop is planted.

## 2. Tutorial

### Player goals

- Learn the basic loop: inspect, water, fertilize or treat problems, advance day, and harvest.
- Understand the difference between credits and Data Seeds.
- Learn that weather, stress, pests, and burning affect outcomes.
- Discover the lab, market, and archives through prompted use rather than free exploration.

### Expected unlocked systems

- Guided orchard actions for watering, nutrients, pest treatment, stress management, and harvest.
- Manual day advancement.
- Weather forecast interpretation.
- First-harvest quest or objective chain.
- Archive unlock on harvested species.
- Lab upgrade purchase after the first Data Seeds are earned.
- Market explanation after the player understands both currencies.

### Rewards

- First harvest payout in credits.
- First Data Seeds payout.
- First archive entry.
- First upgrade discount or guaranteed affordable upgrade.
- Tutorial completion acknowledgement.

### Risks of confusion or drop-off

- Players may not know which plant actions are necessary versus optional.
- Overnight effects can feel random if stress, pests, weather, and growth are not summarized.
- Players may spend resources in the market before understanding upgrades.
- Harvest readiness may be missed if the plant card is not strongly highlighted.

### Boring loops

- Advancing days repeatedly to wait for maturity without meaningful choices.
- Applying every care item whenever possible instead of making strategic decisions.

### Missing feedback

- No after-day recap explaining why each plant changed.
- No “you are ready to harvest” route back to the plant.
- No tutorial checklist showing remaining steps.

### Missing rewards

- No milestone bundle for finishing the first loop.
- No cosmetic, title, or profile marker for becoming a certified grower.

### Progression weaknesses

- The first upgrade may not feel connected to a specific pain point the player just experienced.
- Tutorial completion may not branch into clear next goals.

### Suggested improvements

- Implement a guided first-harvest quest with explicit objective states and reward claiming.
- Add a daily recap panel listing growth gained, weather impact, stress changes, and new problems.
- Guarantee the first harvest grants enough Data Seeds for one entry-level lab upgrade.
- Use contextual prompts that explain why an action matters before the player clicks it.
- End the tutorial by offering three goals: expand orchard, complete archive entries, or improve weather resilience.

## 3. First hour

### Player goals

- Build a small crop plan instead of planting randomly.
- Earn enough credits to keep planting and start saving for orchard expansion.
- Spend Data Seeds on upgrades that visibly improve the next cycle.
- Unlock several archive entries and understand collection progress.
- Learn when to use market conversion and when to save resources.

### Expected unlocked systems

- Multiple plant species with different costs, yields, growth times, and risk profiles.
- Lab upgrades for stress resistance, pest defense, growth speed, or care efficiency.
- Market exchange or resource conversion.
- Rankings visibility for long-term comparison.
- Profile and persistence reminders.
- Basic event/log history.

### Rewards

- New crop discoveries and archive lore.
- First meaningful upgrade purchase.
- Credit surplus large enough to choose between more seeds and orchard unlock saving.
- Visible faster, safer, or more profitable growth from upgrades.

### Risks of confusion or drop-off

- Players may not know which crop is best for their current goal.
- Upgrade math can be opaque if improvements are not shown as before/after effects.
- Rankings can demotivate new players if shown before they have personal milestones.
- Market conversion may undermine lab progression if presented as equally good too early.

### Boring loops

- Repeating the same starter crop because alternatives lack clear roles.
- Cycling plant-care-day-harvest without intermediate events, requests, or surprises.
- Checking rankings without actionable steps to improve.

### Missing feedback

- No crop comparison summary for profit, growth duration, risk, and archive value.
- No forecast-based recommendation such as “heatwave tomorrow; consider resilient crops.”
- No clear next milestone tracker after tutorial completion.

### Missing rewards

- No first-upgrade celebration beyond a log line.
- No collection-tier reward for unlocking several species.
- No personal best or efficiency reward for improved harvests.

### Progression weaknesses

- The first hour can become a linear currency grind.
- Crop choice needs stronger strategic identity.
- Systems exist side by side, but the player may not see how they compound.

### Suggested improvements

- Add crop role labels: starter, fast-cycle, resilient, risky high-yield, weather specialist, and collector crop.
- Add upgrade previews with “current effect → next effect” and recommended first upgrades.
- Add first-hour milestone cards for first upgrade, three archive entries, and first full orchard.
- Add lightweight daily contracts that ask for specific crop outcomes.
- Add forecast-aware tips that connect weather to planting decisions.

## 4. First day

### Player goals

- Form a return plan: what to plant next, what to save for, and what to unlock.
- Unlock or clearly progress toward the second orchard.
- Understand account persistence, profile value, and resource safety.
- Try enough systems to know the game has depth beyond the starter loop.

### Expected unlocked systems

- Orchard unlocking and active orchard selection.
- Longer-term upgrade planning.
- Market, transfer, and rankings literacy.
- Archive completion tracking.
- Climate control introduction, or at least visibility as an aspirational system.
- Global or account stats.

### Rewards

- Second orchard unlock or visible progress toward it.
- New crop tiers from earned credits.
- Archive percentage progress.
- Profile statistics that reflect the first day’s work.
- Possibly a daily login or session completion bonus.

### Risks of confusion or drop-off

- If the second orchard is too expensive relative to early earnings, expansion feels distant.
- If it unlocks too quietly, expansion does not feel like a major achievement.
- Players may not understand why they should return tomorrow besides continuing the same loop.
- Resource transfer and rankings may feel disconnected from orchard play.

### Boring loops

- Filling all available plots with the same optimal crop.
- Advancing days only to convert harvests into more seeds.
- Treating every weather forecast the same because mitigation is too binary or too expensive.

### Missing feedback

- No end-of-session summary with earned credits, Data Seeds, discoveries, losses avoided, and next recommendations.
- No visible progress bar toward orchard unlocks or archive tiers.
- No reminder of unfinished plants, risky forecasts, or available upgrades before logout.

### Missing rewards

- No first-day completion reward.
- No “return tomorrow” setup reward such as a queued contract, forecast bonus, or rested orchard buff.
- No reward for unlocking another orchard beyond capacity.

### Progression weaknesses

- Expansion is capacity-focused, not identity-focused.
- The first day lacks a capstone that says the player graduated from beginner play.

### Suggested improvements

- Add a first-day capstone objective that grants an orchard expansion voucher, rare seed, or lab module.
- Add an end-of-session recap and “recommended next session” list.
- Make each orchard unlock introduce a new modifier, biome, crop family, or management problem.
- Add daily contracts with expiry timers and varied objectives.
- Add non-punitive comeback rewards that help but do not punish missed days.

## 5. Mid game

### Player goals

- Specialize around preferred strategies: safe production, rare crop discovery, weather mastery, market profit, or collection.
- Manage multiple orchards efficiently.
- Unlock higher-tier upgrades and crop types.
- React to weather forecasts proactively.
- Start pursuing leaderboards, archive tiers, and self-directed optimization.

### Expected unlocked systems

- Multiple orchards with distinct capacities or modifiers.
- Broader crop catalog and rarer species.
- Lab upgrade tiers with meaningful tradeoffs.
- Climate control or weather mitigation.
- Contracts, achievements, or collection milestones.
- Stronger market loops and possible NPC merchant hooks.

### Rewards

- Orchard unlock celebrations.
- Rare crop entries and lore reveals.
- Larger harvest payouts.
- Mastery badges for crop families, clean harvests, and weather survival.
- Upgrade milestones that visibly change care frequency or crop resilience.

### Risks of confusion or drop-off

- Managing multiple orchards can become busywork without batch actions or summaries.
- Upgrade paths may have obvious best choices and weak build identity.
- Weather control can feel like a tax if it only prevents bad outcomes.
- Players may hit a content wall once they understand the math.

### Boring loops

- Manual care across many plots with no planning layer.
- Repeating the most profitable crop because contracts, traits, and crop roles do not disrupt the optimum.
- Farming currency solely to buy numerical upgrades.

### Missing feedback

- No multi-orchard dashboard showing which plots need attention.
- No explanation of opportunity costs between upgrades, orchard unlocks, and market use.
- No long-range planning view for forecasts, contracts, and harvest timing.

### Missing rewards

- No specialization titles or build-defining perks.
- No bonus for balanced crop portfolios or weather-specific success.
- No medium-term narrative rewards.

### Progression weaknesses

- Mid-game depth depends on numbers increasing rather than new decision types.
- Multiple orchards expand workload faster than they expand strategy.
- Climate and market systems need more positive incentives.

### Suggested improvements

- Add an orchard command center with alerts, batch care options, and projected harvest timing.
- Add upgrade branches with tradeoffs, such as speed versus resilience or profit versus archive discovery.
- Add rotating contracts that reward underused crops and risky weather plans.
- Add weather mastery rewards that turn storms, fog, or heatwaves into opportunities.
- Add mid-game NPC or lab narrative arcs that unlock through archive progress.

## 6. Late game

### Player goals

- Complete most archive entries and optimize rare-species production.
- Maximize orchard output without excessive manual repetition.
- Finish high-tier upgrades and climate tools.
- Compete on rankings or personal best metrics.
- Solve advanced production puzzles.

### Expected unlocked systems

- Full or near-full crop roster.
- Most orchards unlocked.
- Advanced lab upgrades.
- Climate control with strategic use cases.
- Achievements or mastery badges.
- Stronger economic goals, rare seed sources, and possibly automation.

### Rewards

- High-value harvests and rare Data Seed payouts.
- Late-tier archive lore and completion bonuses.
- Visible prestige for mastered species, perfect harvests, or leaderboard rank.
- Automation or quality-of-life tools that reduce repetitive care.

### Risks of confusion or drop-off

- If the only remaining goal is grinding expensive upgrades, motivation drops.
- Large orchards may overwhelm players with repeated clicks.
- Rare outcomes can feel unfair without pity, targeting, or progress indicators.
- Leaderboards can feel unreachable without leagues or personal-best framing.

### Boring loops

- Full-orchard maintenance with identical actions across many plots.
- Harvest-sell-upgrade repetition with little variance.
- Waiting for rare weather or rare crops without control levers.

### Missing feedback

- No late-game dashboard for efficiency, crop quality, and missed value.
- No clear odds or progress toward rare discoveries.
- No comparison between current build and alternate strategies.

### Missing rewards

- No prestige cosmetics, titles, or orchard visual upgrades.
- No reward for completing difficult constraints such as no-burn streaks or storm harvests.
- No late-game story payoff.

### Progression weaknesses

- Late-game upgrades may flatten into incremental percentage improvements.
- The game needs more horizontal goals once vertical unlocks are nearly complete.

### Suggested improvements

- Add automation tiers, but keep them as planning tools rather than full idle-game replacement.
- Add rare-species research projects with explicit progress meters.
- Add challenge orchards with modifiers and curated rewards.
- Add leagues, personal bests, and seasonal ladders for fairer ranking motivation.
- Add visible orchard customization rewards tied to mastery.

## 7. End game

### Player goals

- Complete the main archive or a defined pinnacle research objective.
- Demonstrate mastery across crops, weather, upgrades, and orchard management.
- Receive narrative and systemic closure.
- Decide whether to continue into post-game challenges.

### Expected unlocked systems

- Complete archive tracking and completion claims.
- Final research tier or prestige project.
- End-game challenge objectives.
- Permanent profile recognition.
- Optional reset, prestige, or seasonal continuation path.

### Rewards

- Completion ceremony, story scene, or final lab breakthrough.
- Permanent title, cosmetic, profile frame, or orchard monument.
- Powerful but non-breaking post-game unlock.
- Summary of career stats: days played, species harvested, credits earned, Data Seeds spent, weather survived, and best harvests.

### Risks of confusion or drop-off

- Players may not know what counts as “finished.”
- The final objective may feel like another grind instead of a culmination.
- Completion can reduce motivation if no post-game path is signposted.

### Boring loops

- Repeating late-game profit loops to fill the last few completion gaps.
- Waiting for rare conditions without alternate paths.

### Missing feedback

- No explicit end-game checklist.
- No final progress framing that distinguishes required, optional, and mastery goals.
- No strong acknowledgement when the player reaches completion.

### Missing rewards

- No capstone reward that changes the player’s identity or orchard presentation.
- No permanent marker of completion visible in rankings or profile.

### Progression weaknesses

- End-game is not currently defined as a destination.
- Completion criteria need to connect collection, mastery, and research rather than only currency.

### Suggested improvements

- Define a pinnacle objective, such as completing the botanical archive and synthesizing a final world-tree specimen.
- Add an end-game checklist with required and optional objectives.
- Add a completion ceremony and permanent profile title.
- Add alternate completion routes for collection-focused, weather-focused, and economy-focused players.
- Unlock post-game challenge modes immediately after completion.

## 8. Post game

### Player goals

- Keep experimenting with optimized builds and unusual crop/weather combinations.
- Chase optional mastery, cosmetics, rankings, and seasonal challenges.
- Revisit the orchard without feeling punished for taking breaks.
- Share accomplishments through profile and leaderboard identity.

### Expected unlocked systems

- Repeatable contracts, challenge orchards, seasonal ladders, and personal-best tracking.
- Prestige research or soft-reset options if appropriate.
- Cosmetic orchard customization.
- Advanced automation and batch planning.
- Expanded ranking categories beyond total currency.

### Rewards

- Cosmetic variants, profile badges, titles, and orchard monuments.
- Seasonal or challenge-specific rewards.
- Prestige currency or research tiers that unlock new strategies.
- Personal-best records and milestone recaps.

### Risks of confusion or drop-off

- Repeatable goals can feel like chores if rewards are only numerical.
- Competitive rankings may alienate players who prefer solo completion.
- Prestige can feel punishing if reset benefits are unclear.
- Live-style daily goals can create anxiety if missed days matter too much.

### Boring loops

- Infinite farming for leaderboard position.
- Repeating the same optimal late-game build every season.
- Maintaining huge orchards after all meaningful unlocks are complete.

### Missing feedback

- No seasonal summary or challenge history.
- No recommendation engine for underplayed crops, unearned badges, or alternate builds.
- No clear distinction between optional prestige goals and core completion.

### Missing rewards

- No recurring cosmetic track.
- No advanced collection variants or expert-only crop mutations.
- No social recognition for different play styles.

### Progression weaknesses

- Post-game requires horizontal variety, not just larger numbers.
- The game needs multiple repeatable motivations: mastery, creativity, competition, and discovery.

### Suggested improvements

- Add seasonal challenge boards with solo, friends, and global categories.
- Add prestige research that unlocks variants without invalidating completed progress.
- Add cosmetic orchard customization earned through specific mastery feats.
- Add optional challenge modifiers, such as storm season, low-water orchards, no-market runs, and rare-crop expeditions.
- Add comeback bonuses that encourage return play without punishing absence.

## Cross-stage priorities

| Priority | Improvement | Stages helped | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Guided objective chain from planting through first upgrade | Game start, tutorial, first hour | Reduces early confusion and proves the core loop quickly. |
| 2 | Daily recap and next-session recommendations | Tutorial, first hour, first day, mid game | Converts hidden simulation changes into readable learning. |
| 3 | Crop roles and forecast-aware recommendations | First hour, first day, mid game | Makes planting choices strategic instead of arbitrary. |
| 4 | Milestone rewards and mastery badges | All stages | Keeps progression emotionally rewarding, not just numerical. |
| 5 | Orchard dashboard and batch planning | Mid game, late game, post game | Prevents scale from turning into repetitive clicking. |
| 6 | Defined end-game and post-game paths | Late game, end game, post game | Gives players a destination and reasons to return after completion. |
